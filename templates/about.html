Tips for Best Results

  • Ensure good lighting - well-lit faces provide better detection accuracy
  • Use front-facing photos - side profiles are harder to analyze
  • Keep faces clearly visible - avoid blurry or heavily filtered images
  • Maintain appropriate distance - not too close or too far from the camera
  • Use high-quality images - higher resolution leads to better results
  • Avoid multiple faces - single face detection works best

Dataset Information

Our model is trained on the FER2013 (Facial Expression Recognition 2013) dataset, which contains 35,887 grayscale images of 48x48 pixels. The dataset includes facial expressions categorized into seven emotions: Happy, Sad, Angry, Fear, Surprise, Disgust, and Neutral. Each image is labeled by human annotators, ensuring high-quality training data for accurate emotion recognition.

Privacy & Security

We prioritize your privacy and security. All image processing is done locally on our servers, and uploaded images are automatically deleted after processing. We do not store or share any personal data. The live detection feature uses your device's camera but does not record or store any video footage.
